Describe at least two ethical issues related to AI or cybersecurity that could arise in this career/industry

  • A computer can’t be held accountable. There are a lot of AI management things being developed as of late; What happens when an LLM gives bad advice? Who should be held accountable?
  • Not only does AI take jobs from people, but a lot of the time there is a lot of detail/nuance that is lost compared to interacting with a person. For example, I’ve found that many people consult AI for advice when starting a new hobby and roll with whatever their LLM of choice spits out. This tends to be general advice that is often missing a few important bits and pieces and occasionally even has misinformation sprinkled in there every now and then. A lot of people higher up on the corporate chain see AI as a 1:1 replacement for certain types of jobs when that just is not the case at all.

How does your personal values shape how you handle each issue?

  • Logically, the first thing makes sense because accountability is what keeps processes in check and things running smoothly. Without a specific person or group taking on the responsibility of solving an issue, Things become disorderly and people become complacent, leading to a collapse of sorts in organizational structure.
  • Trust but verify is huge for the second thing. Rather than treating AI as a complete replacement for certain jobs, it is better to use it as a tool to assist us in matters outside of being autonomous.
